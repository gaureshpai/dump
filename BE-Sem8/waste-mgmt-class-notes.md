## Module 1: Evolution, Souces, Types and Generation of Solid Waste

### Vid 1: Introduction to Solid Waste

Solid waste comprises all the waste arising from human and animal activities that are typically solid and discarded as useless or unwanted.

Some of the commonly used synonyms for solid waste are:

- Refuse
- Garbage
- Rubbish
- Scrap
- Debris

The recent year solid waste affected events were:

- Plastic burst in Mumbai 2005
- Chennai dump yard crash 2015
- Blast in steel factory Noida caused by the imported scrap from Iran
- Reduction in number of migration birds
- Stray animals dying
- Fires of Mumbai daily dump sites

Speaking about management of waste in old India, we have an article from _Manusmriti_, which is the first systematic treatment of Hindu law. It also prohibited throwing of dust, garbage, rubbish, pieces of meat on the highway or water bodies and made it punishable. Also, Kautilya's _Artha Shastra_ and _Ayurveda_ prevented it.

Speaking about management of waste in New India, a few of the examples are like Surat Plague.

First law for municipal solid waste management and handling rules was in 1987.

A PIL at the Supreme Court in 1991 was filed by Al Mitra Patel.

In 2000, _Manusmriti_ calls solid waste management and handling tools for revised.

Waste collection rate:
Waste collected includes contaminants,

- Commingled waste
- Source separated waste.

Effect of source reduction on waste generation:

- Decreases unnecessary or excessive packaging
- Substitutes reusable products for disposable single-use products

Effect of Public Attitudes and Legislation on Waste Generation

1. Public attitude. Significant reduction in waste generation can be observed when people take initiatives on their own to conserve natural resources and reduce economical burdens associated with solid waste management.
2. Legal laws and legislation. It is one of the important factors affecting the generation of certain types of wastes.
3. Encouraging the purchase of use of recycled materials by allowing differential pricing.
